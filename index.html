Tell me about a time you had to deal with a difficult stakeholder.
Ace is answering

At JPMorgan, a senior analyst kept bypassing the data team — pulling raw tables directly from Redshift and building his own Excel reports. His numbers were diverging from official dashboards because he was hitting unprocessed data before our transformation logic ran.

Rather than escalating it as a process violation, I sat with him and walked through exactly where his numbers differed. Once he saw the specific transformation steps he was missing — currency normalization, transaction deduplication — he understood the gap. I built him a dedicated view matching his workflow but pulling from the curated layer.

Amazon's ownership principle is exactly that — meeting stakeholders where they are, not enforcing process from above.
